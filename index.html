Introduction

What is a Stroke?

A lack of blood flow within the brain, often caused by blockage or haemorrhage, which causes a lack of oxygen and nutrients to brain tissue. This leads to damage to the affected part of the brain, and can eventually lead to death of the tissue if they are deprived of oxygen for too long.

There are two types of stroke

Ischemic Stroke
  • Most Common (~80%)
  • Blockage caused by a blood clot
Hemorrhagic stroke
  • Less common (~20%)
  • Cause: Artery burst within the brain causing blood to leak out, increasing pressure within the skull
