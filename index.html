Introduction

What is a Stroke?

A lack of blood flow within the brain, often caused by blockage or haemorrhage, which causes a lack of oxygen and nutrients to brain tissue. This leads to damage to the affected part of the brain, and can eventually lead to death of the tissue if they are deprived of oxygen for too long.

There are two types of stroke

Ischemic Stroke
  • Most Common (~80%)
  • Cause: Blockage caused by a blood clot due to
    ×

    Atherosclerosis
    Plaque build up due to high levels of 'bad' cholesterol causes narrowing, which will eventually lead to clot formation. Plaque can also break off peripheral build ups and travel to the brain, leading to a blockage.

    Thrombosis
    Blood clot formation within the vessels, which can travel into the brain and cause blockage
Hemorrhagic stroke
  • Less common (~20%)
  • Cause: Artery burst within the brain causing blood to leak out, increasing pressure within the skull
